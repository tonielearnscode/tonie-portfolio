Modernizing Design Elements

Strategic Makeover:
The Art and Science of Website Revamp

In the ever-evolving digital landscape, a successful online presence is crucial for businesses to thrive. This case study
delves into the redesign of Petnificent, a pet grooming website, focusing on enhancing the user experience
to create a more engaging and efficient platform for pet owners.

My goals? An app that attracts people to use the website more through improved interface design & interactive features.

Project Type: Passion Project
My Role: UX UI Designer

Duration: 70 hours
Tools: Figma, Photoshop, Miro

Design Challenge

Petnificent could benefit from a responsive website design. The overall website should be informative and friendly.

  • Users want to find a good deal/ honest prices, websites that don’t look dated, and the ability to schedule services
  • Design a voter registration app that helps user to book appointments, easily find costs, prevent overbooking

Design Goal

An app that attracts users to use the website more through improved interface design & interactive features.

User Research Summary

I began the project by using Heuristic Evaluation while looking at the original site. Following these principles, the website’s main usability problems were a lack of:

  • #8 Aesthetic and minimalist design
  • #6 Recognition Rather than Recall
  • #9 Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors

After this evaluation, it was clear that there were a few usability problems that could be solved with user research and better UI design. I decided to focus the redesign on the layout and UI design, and keep the original information architecture.

Voices of the Users: The Heart of Our Problem Solving

2 of the 5 people I interviewed gave me similar insights so here are the main insights:


Why do users book professional pet groomers?

  • too much cleaning at home
  • dog is difficult to handle
  • clipping nails is hard
  • the fur smells good longer
  • pet needs to look nice for events

What features do users want when booking a groomer?

  • deals/promos
  • reviews from other users
  • cost of services
  • availability and walk in options

Why do users wash their own dogs?

  • saves money
  • can double check own dog for illness
  • dog is low maintenance so its not worth a professional groomer
  • a way to bond with their dog and satisfying to do

Turning Pain Points into Smooth Sailing:

Interviews showed 4 typical worries:

Navigation & Layout:
  • Menu has unclear information hierarchy or lack of breadcrumbs
Lack of Support:
  • No FAQs to answer common questions
Pay Online:
  • No credibility shown with a modern and secure site
Accessibility
  • The color and contrast creates barriers for users who have low vision, color blindness, or other visual impairments
